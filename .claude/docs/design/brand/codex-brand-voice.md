# Codex: Guardia Brand Voice

> **Prefix:** `codex-` | **Type:** Reference Manual | **Scope:** All written and verbal communication on behalf of Guardia

## Content

### Voice pillars

#### 1. Direct

We get to the point. We structure arguments with logic and precision; clarity over sophistication.

> *"Manual accounting consumes 40% of the team's time. Guardia gives that time back."*

#### 2. Strategic

Every communication serves a decision. Each text guides the reader's next step with context, trade-off, and direction.

> *"Before scaling the operation, map the 20% of transactions that generate 80% of the exceptions. That's where automation returns the most time and reduces the most risk."*

#### 3. Affirmative

We affirm what we are. "Not X, but Y" constructions force processing of the negation before the proposal. The proposal comes first.

> *"Guardia operates, audits, and closes. Every entry traceable from origin to ledger."*

#### 4. Clear

We write to be understood on first read. We translate technical terms when necessary; common language when not.

> *"Guardia agents run accounting routines end-to-end (for example, reconciliation crossing multiple sources in a single run) and deliver compliance-ready audit trails."*

### Personality

| Attribute | What this means |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Confident | We speak with technical authority and respect for the interlocutor |
| Close | We treat clients as partners, within a professional tone |
| Practical | Each intervention points to real outcomes |
| Ambitious | Big vision sustained by concrete execution |
| Precise | Clear technical language with human warmth |
| Vigilant | We follow detail and context with analytical calm |
| Inclusive | Every voice and perspective takes part in building the brand |

### Practical rules

#### Clarity and structure

- Simple frameworks: *Why → What → How*; or *Problem → Cause → Solution*.
- Back claims with numbers, evidence, and verifiable references.
- One idea per sentence; one sentence per line break when density requires.

#### Vocabulary

- Technical vocabulary only when needed, always with concept translation.
- Eliminate empty buzzwords: *innovative, disruptive, transformative, revolutionary*.
- Action verbs over nominalizations ("reconcile" instead of "performing reconciliation").

#### Punctuation and rhythm

- Parentheses for nuance within a sentence.
- Ellipses for interruption or continuation.
- Avoid em dashes and colons to introduce concepts.

#### Positioning

- Guardia is **agentic accounting**, not fintech.
- Sequence when describing capabilities: **accounting → financial → tax → fiscal**.
- Avoid "revolutionize/transform" without concrete result.

### Examples

#### Positioning

✅ "Guardia is an agentic accounting platform that automates accounting, financial, tax, and fiscal operations with AI agents auditing every move."

❌ "Guardia is an innovative infrastructure revolutionizing the accounting market with disruptive AI solutions."

#### Results

✅ "We reduced monthly close time by 70% for an accounting firm with 5,000 clients."

❌ "We deliver transformative operational efficiency to our clients."

#### Technology

✅ "Guardia agents run the accounting routine end-to-end. They read the data, apply the rules, classify and reconcile entries, with full traceability."

❌ "Our state-of-the-art AI uses advanced algorithms to revolutionize how accounting is done."

#### Affirmative structure

✅ "Guardia is direction, motion, and progress."

❌ "Guardia is not a static fit; it is direction."

### Channel applications

| Channel | Posture | Structure | Watch out |
|---------|---------|-----------|-----------|
| LinkedIn / public positioning | Confident, strategic, insight-oriented | Thesis → argument with data → conclusion/invitation | Generic lists, empty motivational, emoji excess are out |
| Site / institutional | Precise, clear, human | Direct value proposition → concrete benefits → social proof | Brochure language ("complete solutions") is out |
| Commercial email / proposals | Direct, decision-oriented | Context → proposal → next step | Long preambles, self-praise, vague closings are out |
| Technical documentation / product | Precise, didactic, enabling | What it is → when to use → how to do | Context-free explanations, untranslated jargon are out |
| Support | Close, responsible, resolutive | Acknowledge → cause → solution | Generic answers, blame-shifting are out |
| Internal communication | Transparent, direct, alignment-driven | Context → decision/info → impact for the team | "Synergy," "alignment" as empty noun are out |

### Review checklist

Before publishing, confirm:

- [ ] The text helps the reader make a decision or move the discussion.
- [ ] Claims are backed by data, evidence, or references.
- [ ] Every term carries concrete meaning.
- [ ] Statements are direct, in positive form.
- [ ] The **agentic accounting** positioning is preserved.
- [ ] The sequence **accounting → financial → tax → fiscal** is respected when applicable.
- [ ] The text fits the channel and target audience.
